# Context and Acknowledgments

This essay was developed within the framework of the course *Philosophical Perspectives on the Exact Sciences*. Our main interest lies in the ontology of rods and clocks, highlighting a potential "loophole" in the theory of General Relativity. To explore this, we dive into the historical debate between Hermann Weyl and Albert Einstein on the matter.

Beyond the essay itself, I would like to emphasize the importance of the discussions held alongside this work, which were, to me, the most rewarding part of the project. Special thanks go to Prof. Troyanov, Prof. Moschidis, Prof. Bossoney, and our supervisor, PhD candidate Amine Rusi El Hassani, for their invaluable insights and the engaging debates that deeply guided this research.
